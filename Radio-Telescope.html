Radio Telescope Project

I had an opprotunity to contribute to a student led project for Fermilab. The Society of Women Engineers at the College of Dupage were given the task to create a device that could measure and output the voltage of a charging solar panel. Using an Arduino, a Raspberry Pi, and knowledge of elementary circuit analyis we were able to create such a device. My main role was helping write the Python code. For example I helped make the Raspberry Pi read the voltage and convert it from a 5 volt range to a 12 volt range. Below is a picture of some of the code and an output from a test on a battery.

Fellow member Joey contributed a lot to the Python code as well and was critical in trouble shooting the Raspberry Pi.


This picture shows the circuit, Arduino, and Raspberry Pi. Along with coding, I would trouble shoot the circuit and set up the device to work with a solar panel and car battery.


After we had tested our device with numerous small batteries, we were ready to attach it to a car battery. The car battery was being charged from the solar power below. Our code would output the voltage of the battery on a computer screen so we could get real time feed back on the charging process. This would allow us to quickly spot any irregularities and ensure both the solar panel and battery were functioning as needed.

Overall, this was a great project and I am thankful for having the opprotunity to help out with it. When I was attending the College of Dupage, The Society of Women Engineers was the group I enjoyed working with the most. I was able to work both individually and in a group, and got to work in areas I had little experience in at the time.
