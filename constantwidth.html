Curves of Constant Width

A curve of constant width is a curve with the feature that two parallel tangents to the curve is constant. The best known curve of constant width is a circle. It's width (diameter), does not change when rotated. Another curve of constant width is the Reuleaux triangle. A Reuleaux triangle can be made by drawing a circle centred at one edge of an equilateral triangle and ending at another end of said triangle. When done for all three sides of the triangle, what is inscribed is a shape of constant width.

Solids of Constant Width

Solids of constant width is a surface with constant distance between two opposite tangent parallel planes. A solid of constant width can be made by revolving a curve of constant width around on of its axes of symmetry. Just how revolving a circle around its axis traces a sphere, rotating a Reuleaux triangle traces a solid of constant width. Below I modeled using PTC Creo and had it 3D printed.
